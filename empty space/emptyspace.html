Empty space, dark matter and the behaviour of light in a vaccuum are frequently the subject of study in physics. However, so far, empty space has not been found.
Nearly a hundred years ago, in 1928, the mathematician Paul Dirac was playing with equations when he msde a startling discovery - Empty space is not empty. It is teemimg with activity - the creation and annihilation of matter and antimatter, too quick for the human senses to percieve.

Shivani's thoughts turned to the Upanishads - a five thousamd year old Indian text which echo the same thought. "Kha"is a Sanskrit word that is loosely translated into English as "nothing", but perhaps a better translation is: "that unmanifest and unseen potential that has extensive capabilities".
To understand Kha we can think of the hole in an axel wheel, the gap , the 'nothing space' in which an axel is inserted. Turning this axel drives the wheel forward.

The route to infinity is via Nothing. A thought that is echoed throughout the Upanishads.
