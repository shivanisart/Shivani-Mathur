The pathway from Lucknow to London

Originally from India, Shivani Mathur came to London in June 1998 on an allowance of 35 pounds per day, expecting to spend two weeks here. Financial markets were in turmoil, her education background seemed 'just right' and she found opportunity in the male dominated realm of investment banking. Entering this high-stakes, high-emotion environment, everyone was relentlessly chasing their goals.

​

She soon rose to be recognised as the top two percent of women in the financial sector in London, and she loved her job, but something in her was restless to look beyond. As an outsider,her vision was of a world where extraordinary opportunities are accessible to, ordinary people with big dreams, grit, humility, an incessant quest for learning. People who do not shy from taking alternate pathways.

How Art entered her life

Shivani realized that art has the power to empower, provoke thought, and bring about positive change. Thus, alongside her corporate career, she embarked on a parallel artistic journey, igniting conversations around disruption, opportunity, social justice, and sustainability. During the pandemic she enrolled in a Master’s degree in Art and Science at Central Saint Martins, London. This transformative experience earned her a distinction and an award for sustainability by the prestigious 'Maison Zero - a joint award by LVMH and the University of the Arts, London. Her research paper titled "Pregnant with infinity" was peer reviewed and published by Leonardo labs.

​

Inspiration

Her current work is directly influenced by resaearch in three distinct disciplines

- Ancient Indian philosophy from the Upanishads, emphasizing self-discovery and interconnectedness, encouraging individuals to look within themselves to understand the universe.
- The Japanese art of Kintsugi, celebrating imperfections and breakage.
- Quantum physics, which explores subatomic particle behavior and questions how they are related to the cosmic scale.

At the core of her creative practice lies the belief that art transforms lives.

In Shivani's studio

In her studio, Shivani immerses herself in clay, paint, slime mould and discarded materials like broken mirrors and eggshells. Each work explores subtle cracks symbolizing life's disruptions and the lack of agency we have over many aspects of our lives. These cracks evolve unpredictably over time and begin to reflect, transmit, and amplify light. Her artwork celebrates cracks and imperfections as pathways of light. Light awakens the dormant potential within each and every one of us. Potential that can empower us to see the invisible, touch the intangible, and achieve the seemingly impossible.

Everything in the studio seems to have a mind of its own. Yet somehow, it is a sanctuary of calmness....As if there is a structure in the chaos.
